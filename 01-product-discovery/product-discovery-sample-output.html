1. Discovery Brief

What decision are we trying to make?

Determine whether improving content discovery inside the mobile sales experience should become a near-term product priority.

Problem statement: For field sales reps preparing for client meetings, when they need account-relevant content in the mobile app, they struggle to find the right case study, deck, product sheet, or objection-handling material because content is fragmented, weakly tagged, and not surfaced in the context of the meeting.

Why It Matters

Reps spend meeting prep time searching, reusing stale content, or asking peers for assets. This can reduce message quality, slow selling motion, and weaken customer confidence during high-value conversations.

  • More prep time spent searching than tailoring.
  • Higher risk of outdated or off-brand material.
  • Lower confidence in account-specific messaging.
42% of surveyed field reps said finding the right pre-meeting content is a frequent friction point.
11 min average reported time spent searching for client-ready content before a meeting.
31% of recent mobile content searches ended without a content open or share event.
18% of shared assets were more than 12 months old in the sample account-meeting dataset.

3. Customer And Business Context

Who is affected and what outcome is at stake?

The discovery agent should separate user pain from business impact so the team can make a better product decision.

Primary user

Field Account Executive

Needs to prepare quickly between meetings, tailor the conversation to account context, and share approved content from a mobile-first workflow.

Business partner

Sales Enablement

Needs reps to use current, approved, measurable content instead of stale decks, local copies, or informal peer recommendations.

Business outcome

Meeting Quality

Improve readiness, consistency, and relevance of client conversations without adding administrative burden to the sales motion.

4. Opportunity Areas

Where should the team focus discovery?

These are not feature commitments. They are opportunity areas that should be tested with users and data.

Opportunity User Value Business Value Main Risk Next Learning Step
Meeting-aware content recommendations Surface relevant assets from account, industry, opportunity stage, product interest, and meeting objective. Increase use of approved content and improve consistency of field messaging. Recommendations may be noisy if CRM data or content metadata is incomplete. Run concept test with 12 reps using real upcoming meetings.

Metadata work may be operationally expensive and slow without clear ownership. Audit top 100 shared assets and define minimum viable tagging standard.
5. Assumptions And Risks

What must be true for this to be worth solving?

The discovery agent should make uncertainty explicit before the product team moves into solution definition.

Highest-Risk Assumptions

  • Reps would use recommended content if surfaced inside the meeting workflow.
  • CRM meeting data is complete enough to infer useful content needs.
  • Content metadata can be improved without creating a heavy manual process.
  • Meeting content quality has measurable impact on sales outcomes or rep efficiency.
  • Sales enablement can maintain trust signals such as approved, current, and recommended.

Evidence Gaps

  • Actual behavior during mobile meeting prep, not just self-reported friction.
  • Which content attributes matter most by segment, industry, and sales stage.
  • How often reps use external channels because mobile search fails.
  • Whether reps want recommendations, better search, stronger filters, or peer proof.
  • Which business metric should anchor prioritization: rep time, content reuse, meeting quality, or pipeline influence.
6. Recommended Discovery Plan

Two-week learning sprint

Recommended action: move from initial discovery synthesis into focused validation before committing to a feature direction.

1. Observe Real Prep

Watch 8 field reps prepare for upcoming meetings on mobile. Capture search behavior, switching, friction, workarounds, and decision points.

2. Test Concepts

Use lightweight prototypes for meeting-aware recommendations, trust labels, and prep-flow placement. Compare comprehension and preference.

3. Audit Content Quality

Review top shared and searched assets for metadata completeness, ownership, freshness, industry tags, persona tags, and stage tags.

4. Prepare Decision Memo

Recommend whether to prioritize recommendation UX, trust signals, metadata cleanup, workflow integration, or a smaller experiment.

7. Success Measures

How we might know the problem is improving

Metrics should be refined after validating which behavior best represents value.

Measure Type Candidate Metric Why It Matters
User behavior Increase in pre-meeting content open or share events from mobile. Shows reps are using content in the moment the product is trying to support.
Efficiency Reduction in self-reported time to find client-ready content. Captures prep burden and field workflow impact.
Quality Decrease in shares of outdated or unapproved assets. Aligns product improvement with enablement governance and message consistency.
Business signal Increase in content-influenced meeting progression for targeted opportunity stages. Connects the discovery area to sales outcomes, but requires careful attribution.
8. Product Decision Recommendation

Move into focused validation, then choose a narrow experiment

The evidence is strong enough to move beyond broad discovery, but not yet strong enough to commit to a specific feature build.

Recommendation

Run a two-week validation sprint before roadmap commitment.

The likely opportunity is not "better search" alone. The stronger hypothesis is that field reps need meeting-aware, trusted, and current content surfaced inside the CRM mobile prep workflow.

Decision After Sprint

  • Prioritize a meeting-aware recommendation prototype.
  • Prioritize trust signals and content governance.
  • Prioritize metadata cleanup before UX investment.
  • Pause if observed behavior does not support the stated problem.
